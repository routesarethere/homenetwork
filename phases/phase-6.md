## Phase 6a — Dec 2023

It took exactly 17 days to realize what I had at my fingertips and that I was going to outgrow it fast. Off to eBay I went to find something low power but capable. I settled on a Lenovo M920Q.

I knew I wanted a solid hypervisor to virtualize things so it would be easier to build, tear down, back up, and restore. Proxmox was the obvious homelab choice, so I immediately wiped Windows and gave it a shot. In a matter of minutes I spun up LXCs for Pi-Hole and Home Assistant using Tteck's helper scripts.

Seeing all the services I could run, I was again a kid in a candy store. The Unifi Network app I had running on a laptop? Easily migrated to an LXC. I could self-host my own photo storage. I could self-host my own media server — like I dreamed of doing when I was 14 years old. I wonder what an external drive to rip discs costs? How difficult is it to rip 4K content? I'm gonna need some real storage.

---

## Phase 6b — Dec 2023

Six days later, I had placed an order for a Synology DS923+, two 4TB drives, and a 4K UHD disc drive.

I had all my favorite childhood movies on DVD but not many other movies, so I figured I'd have plenty of space while I started collecting Blu-rays and worked up to 4K discs in the future. I remember seeing the 4K Blu-ray for Fast X on sale for like $10. They made 10 of these movies? Last I saw was the 6th one. Anyway — cheap enough to use as a test.

I had heard of both Plex and Jellyfin. Not fully understanding transcoding, what Plex Pass entailed, but knowing Jellyfin is totally free, I decided to give it a shot. Awesome! Except for certain media not playing and throwing errors. Old Roku TVs are notorious for being terrible media clients.

While several years old, the Nvidia Shield Pro still held strong as a media client according to the community — and that remote looked really nice. A very worthwhile upgrade.
